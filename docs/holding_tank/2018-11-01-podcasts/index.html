Podcasts

Oh wait Andy has just discovered podcasts so now he has to write a blog post about it? No I've been listening to podcasts for ages, but I haven't blogged about it before.

I really got into podcasts when I started commuting downtown to an office on the bus and needed something to keep me awake. Books are great, but I started switching to podcasts instead. Many years later podcasts keep me company at the gym, when I'm doing household tasks, or on a long drive.

Here's my current favourites:

  • The Allusionist.

  • From Our Own Correspondent.

  • Planet Money and The Indicator.

  • No Such Thing as a Fish.

  • 99% Invisible.

  • Imaginary Worlds.

  • Radiolab.

  • More or Less.

Notable podcasts I've stopped listening too:

  • West Wing Weekly. If you are a big West Wing fan (I am) you'll enjoy this episode by episode dissection. But I wanted to start re-watching each episode and then listening to the podcast. So I stopped listening and focused on watching and listening at the same time. Then I stopped watching and quite frankly I've had enough of US politics. I enjoyed it and I'll pick it up again one day.

  • Freakonomics. A reliable friend for many years, but I found the podcast getting too long on particular subjects. There were some episodes that just went on too long. The novelty has worn off and I get my economics fix elsewhere these days.
